Stages of Cancer





It is the process of determining how far cancer has progressed and spread in the body. Progression is determined by assigning a number from 1 to 4, where 1 means that the cancer has been isolated, and 4 means that the cancer has spread to the extent that it can be evaluated.

zero phase (0) :
It is the first stage, which indicates that there is no cancer, but there is an abnormal growth of cells, which is likely to turn into cancer.

Stage one (I) :
This is an early stage of cancer, which means a small tumor is confined to one specific area of ​​the body.

Stage Two (II) :
During which the tumor has grown larger, but has not yet spread to other areas of the body.

The third stage (III) :
During which the tumor has grown larger, but has not yet spread to other areas of the body.

The fourth stage (IV) :
If this stage of cancer is diagnosed, it means that the disease has spread to other parts of the body, and at this stage it is called secondary cancer.

What Is Lung Cancer?

Lung Cancer is a type of cancer that starts in the lungs. Cancer starts when cells in the body begin to grow out of control. To learn more about how cancers start and spread, see What Is Cancer?

Colon Cancer

What Is Colon Cancer?

Colon cancer is a type of cancer that begins in the large intestine (colon). The colon is the final part of the digestive tract. Colon cancer typically affects older adults.see What Is Cancer?

Liver Cancer

What is liver cancer?

Liver cancer is a cancer that's found anywhere in the liver. The liver is a large organ at the top right side of your tummy. It helps you digest your food and removes toxins. see What Is Cancer?

Esophageal cancer

What is Esophageal cancer?

is cancer arising from the esophagus—the food pipe that runs between the throat and the stomach.Symptoms often include difficulty in swallowing and weight loss, see What Is Cancer?

Pancreas cancer

What is pancreatic cancer?
